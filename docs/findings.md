# Findings

This page lists every defect surfaced by the v0.6.3.1 A2A campaign that has been filed against [`alphaonedev/ai-memory-mcp`](https://github.com/alphaonedev/ai-memory-mcp). It is the public ledger of what the campaign caught and where each finding is in the Patch 2 funnel.

The page is regenerated by the `findings-sync.yml` workflow on `ai-memory-a2a-v0.6.3.1`. The workflow watches issues on `ai-memory-mcp` labelled `v0.6.3.2-candidate` and parent-linked to the campaign's umbrella tracking issue, and rewrites the *Campaign-discovered findings* section below from that data. Pre-campaign reds are listed manually because they predate the harness.

## Pre-campaign reds (known-open on v0.6.3.1)

These are the two defects the campaign knows about going in. They are encoded as the **expected-red** scenarios `S23` and `S24` so the harness can prove on every run that it can detect them.

| Scenario | Issue | Severity | Title | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `S23` | [`#507` — config.toml `~` expansion](https://github.com/alphaonedev/ai-memory-mcp/issues/507) | medium | config.toml `~` expansion | **Seed defect for Patch 2.** Umbrella tracking issue is anchored on this. |
| `S24` | [`#318` — MCP stdio writes bypass federation fanout](https://github.com/alphaonedev/ai-memory-mcp/issues/318) | high | MCP stdio writes bypass federation fanout | Patch 2 candidate. Asymmetric: reads via stdio are fine, writes silently diverge. |

Both flip to expected-green in the successor `ai-memory-a2a-v0.6.3.2` repo. If either of them somehow comes back green on this v0.6.3.1 campaign, the harness is broken — that is the point of including them.

### Why these two specifically

The two pre-campaign reds were chosen because they are unambiguous, reproducible, and document a concrete user-visible failure mode:

- [`#507`](https://github.com/alphaonedev/ai-memory-mcp/issues/507) is a one-character-class bug (the loader does not expand `~` before passing the path to SQLite). It is a clean integrity check because the failure mode is binary — either the open succeeds or it does not, no flake surface — and the fix is small enough that it could plausibly land in Patch 2 without any other change.
- [`#318`](https://github.com/alphaonedev/ai-memory-mcp/issues/318) is the more serious of the two: a silent correctness break in the federation fanout layer for one specific transport (MCP stdio). It exercises the harness's ability to detect divergence between mesh nodes rather than just per-node failures, which is structurally different from `S23`.

Together they cover both ends of the harness's detection range: a deterministic per-node failure and a multi-node divergence.

## How findings flow

The funnel has four steps:

1. **Detection.** A scenario fails on a campaign run. The runner writes a structured failure record to `runs/<run-id>/<scenario>.json` including the scenario id, the cell, the expected vs. observed verdicts, and a minimal repro hint.
2. **Issue creation.** The `findings-sync.yml` workflow opens (or updates) a `bug` + `v0.6.3.2-candidate` labelled issue on [`alphaonedev/ai-memory-mcp`](https://github.com/alphaonedev/ai-memory-mcp/issues). The issue body links back to the run summary at `https://github.com/alphaonedev/ai-memory-a2a-v0.6.3.1/blob/main/runs/<run-id>/summary.json`.
3. **Parent-link.** Each new issue is parent-linked to the **umbrella tracking issue** for the v0.6.3.1 campaign on `ai-memory-mcp`. The umbrella issue is the canonical view of "everything Patch 2 needs to fix" and rolls up to the **`v0.6.3.2` milestone** on `ai-memory-mcp`.
4. **Closure.** The `v0.6.3.2` milestone is satisfied — and the umbrella tracking issue closes — when every parent-linked issue has a fix merged and the `S23` / `S24` scenarios (plus any campaign-discovered reds) are green on the successor `ai-memory-a2a-v0.6.3.2` repo's first campaign run.

This funnel is deliberately narrow: a finding only enters Patch 2 if it has a scenario that reproduces it, an issue thread on `ai-memory-mcp`, a label, a parent-link, and a milestone. Anything missing one of those does not count.
